Story of my lief

I was born on a rainy Tuesday, the kind where thunder rolls like distant drums and the sky weeps gently. My mother said the rain was a blessing — a sign that I’d grow up with a heart full of empathy and a mind that danced with dreams. Childhood was a patchwork of scraped knees, mango-stained summers, and bedtime stories whispered under mosquito nets. I learned early that silence could be loud, and that sometimes the smallest acts — like sharing your last piece of candy — could mean everything. In school, I was the quiet one. Not shy, just observant. I watched people like pages in a book, learning their rhythms, their fears, their joys. I found solace in writing — scribbling poems on the backs of notebooks, dreaming of worlds where dragons flew and heartbreak healed with a song. Love came like a monsoon — sudden, overwhelming, beautiful. And like the monsoon, it passed. But it left behind green shoots of wisdom and a deeper understanding of myself. I learned that love isn’t always forever, but it’s always worth it. I’ve failed. Oh, I’ve failed spectacularly. Jobs lost, friendships faded, dreams deferred. But each failure was a teacher, and each scar a signature of survival. I’ve learned to laugh at the chaos, to dance in the uncertainty, and to find meaning in the mess. Now, I live with purpose. I chase sunsets, not deadlines. I collect moments, not things. I speak kindly, love fiercely, and forgive often. My story isn’t perfect — but it’s mine. And every chapter, even the painful ones, has made me who I am.
